5 Years From Now

As an average student in class 2025, how do I see myself five years from now?

Since I was a kid, I have always wanted to be one of the most successful chefs. Wait? How is being a chef connected to the IT industry? Of course, nothing! From a family that is financially struggling, getting accepted at a university that has no fees was a big thing. I was lucky, indeed!

But the dream of being one of the most successful chefs didn't work out. I got accepted at PUP Taguig, but the course that I was bumped into was a diploma. in information technology. I was stuck in a decision about whether I would pursue my dream or accept the opportunity I was facing. That 18-year-old back then doesn't know anything about computers. How can she survive?

In the first year of my college life, I enjoy how we assemble and disassemble parts of system units. My first impression was that IT was not that difficult, but then I was wrong. That was when I encountered programming. I'll be honest, programming has always been my enemy. I'm always having a hard time debugging and understanding how some programs work. but I didn't stop. They say IT is a big industry; maybe I am not good at programming; maybe I am just a late bloomer; who knows?

Five years from now, I am praying and manifesting that I'll be sitting in those offices where I can call myself part of this industry. I know that the journey won't be easy, and I aspire to learn more and accept all the challenges I will be facing. Maybe this was never my first choice or my dream. But to be in this situation of life where I can clearly see how we are struggling so much in terms of financial problems, who am I to quit?
